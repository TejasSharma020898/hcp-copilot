🛡️ Safety & Compliance

Rules run before the AI. Always.

Before the AI is even involved, the system runs a checklist of compliance rules in priority order. Hard blocks are non-negotiable — the AI cannot override them, and neither can the rep.

🚫 HARD BLOCK

Rule 1 — Doctor has opted out of all contact

No outreach permitted via any channel. Complete stop. Overrides everything else in the system.

🚫 HARD BLOCK

Rule 2 — Compliance hold is active

An adverse event has been reported, or a regulatory review is underway. Account frozen until medical affairs formally clears it.

⏱ HARD BLOCK

Rule 3 — Contacted within the last 3 days

Minimum contact gap enforced. Like a physician giving a patient time to rest before a follow-up — some gaps are non-negotiable.

⚠️ WARNING

Rule 4 — Contacted within the last 4–7 days

A soft warning is raised and shown to the rep. They are informed but can still proceed with care. The system flags this rather than blocking.

📡 ROUTING

Rule 5 — Which channel is appropriate for this doctor?

System checks stated preference AND actual response history. If a doctor says they prefer email but never opens any, the system recommends switching to phone or in-person instead.

✅ CLEAR

All rules passed

Only when all rules are checked does the system move to Step 2 — finding the right approved content for this doctor.

🏗 Design Decisions & Tradeoffs

Why we built it this way

Every design choice was made deliberately. Here are the key decisions, the reasoning behind them, and what we consciously left out.

🔒

Rules before AI — always

Decision: Compliance logic is hard-coded in rules that run before the AI sees anything.

Why: AI models can make mistakes. Compliance cannot be probabilistic. A hard opt-out must be a hard stop — no edge cases, no exceptions.

📚

Approved content library only

Decision: The AI can only recommend from pre-approved, tagged assets.

Why: Pharma has strict rules about what can be shared with HCPs. This eliminates the risk of the AI suggesting off-label or unapproved materials — the most common compliance failure in AI-assisted sales tools.

👁

Transparent rationale always shown

Decision: Every recommendation includes a written explanation of why.

Why: Reps need to trust and understand the output. If a manager asks "why did you contact this doctor?", the rep has a clear, defensible, auditable answer.

🔍

Missing data is made visible

Decision: Every recommendation lists its assumptions and knowledge gaps.

Why: Hiding uncertainty is dangerous. When reps know what the system doesn't know, they apply their own judgement to fill the gap — rather than over-trusting a black box.

🚫

What we deliberately left out

  • No live CRM integration — synthetic data only for this prototype
  • No trained predictive model — rules + AI reasoning is sufficient for the prototype stage
  • No A/B testing or feedback loops — would require live deployment to be meaningful
  • No multi-rep coordination — single-rep view is cleaner for the demo
  • No mobile app — desktop-first for the prototype, mobile would come in production

Result: A lean, explainable, compliance-safe prototype that demonstrates the core workflow clearly — without over-engineering.

🚀 What Comes Next

From prototype to production

This prototype demonstrates the core workflow. Here is what the next phase would look like — in priority order.

1

Connect to real data

Live CRM integration (Veeva, Salesforce), real HCP prescribing data from IMS/IQVIA, live email open and click signals from marketing platforms, and a live compliance calendar from regulatory systems.

A feedback loop: did the rep act on the recommendation? Did the doctor respond positively? Use those outcomes to improve future scoring. The system gets better with every interaction.

Multiple reps managing overlapping territories, MSL and commercial team alignment, and a manager dashboard showing all HCPs across a region with priority flags.

4

Full compliance audit trail

Every recommendation logged with its full reasoning, content used, and rep action taken. Regulatory submission support for AI-assisted commercial decisions. Integration with pharmacovigilance reporting.

5

Medical affairs extension

An MSL version focused on scientific exchange rather than commercial activity. KOL management, advisory board tracking, and conference planning and follow-up automation.

6

Mobile-first for field reps

Reps are in the field, not at desks. The next version would be a mobile app — pre-loaded with the day's priority doctor recommendations before leaving the office each morning.

The core principle — unchanged from prototype to production

The HCP Engagement Copilot is a reasoning and workflow tool, not a prediction engine. It gives every rep the intelligence of the best rep on the team — faster decisions, safer compliance, and a clear explanation for every move. The human stays in control.
