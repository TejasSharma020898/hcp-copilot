How does the
HCP Engagement Copilot work?

A plain-English guide to the problem we solved and the product we built — no technical background needed.

The World We're Solving For

Pharma reps talk to thousands of doctors. Most of it goes nowhere.

In the pharmaceutical industry, companies employ large teams whose job is to engage with doctors — called Healthcare Professionals (HCPs) — and share information about medicines, digital health tools, and clinical programs. But this process is broken in a very specific way.

🎯

They're guessing who to contact

A rep might have 200 doctors in their territory. With no intelligent system to guide them, they go in order of a spreadsheet — not based on who's actually ready to engage.

📧

They're sending the wrong thing

One doctor loves email. Another never opens them. One prefers face-to-face meetings. One is a webinar person. But the same email goes to everyone anyway.

⚠️

They're ignoring compliance rules

Some doctors have opted out of contact. Some are in a regulatory blackout (e.g., after a drug side effect report). Contacting them anyway is not just ineffective — it's a legal risk.

Think of it this way

Imagine a sales rep at a bank calling every customer on Monday morning to sell them a credit card. No data. No personalisation. Same script for everyone. They call a customer who already opted out. They call someone who just complained. They don't call the customer who's been browsing loans for a week.

That's exactly what's happening in pharma today. Our job was to build the intelligent layer that changes this.

The Solution

An AI Copilot that tells you exactly what to do next — and why.

We built an intelligent assistant that a pharma rep opens in their browser. They click on any doctor's name, and the AI analyses everything it knows about that doctor — then tells the rep: "Here's what you should do, how you should reach out, what content to send, and here's a draft message — ready to go."

The key word is "Copilot" — it's not replacing the rep. It's making the rep 10x smarter before every interaction.
How It Works

5 things happen the moment you click a doctor's name.

Under the hood, the app runs a carefully designed pipeline — every step happens in order, every step matters.

1

It builds a full picture of the doctor

The system pulls together everything it knows: the doctor's specialty, which hospital they work at, how many patients they see, when they were last contacted, how they responded (did they open the email? attend the webinar? click through?), and what they prefer. This becomes a "context snapshot" for the AI to work with.

Data Assembly
2

It checks all the rules — before anything else

This is the most important step, and it happens before the AI even gets involved. A dedicated rules engine checks: Has this doctor opted out? Are they in a compliance blackout? Were they contacted too recently? What channels are they allowed to be reached on? If any hard rule is violated, the system immediately says "Do Not Contact" and explains why. No AI guess-work on compliance — it's rule-based and deterministic.

Rules Engine — Compliance First
3

It finds the most relevant approved content

The company has a library of pre-approved materials — clinical study summaries, patient guides, webinar invites, product overviews. The system looks at the doctor's therapy area (e.g., HIV, Cardiovascular, Endocrinology) and their preferred channel (email, phone, in-person), then retrieves the top 3–4 most relevant approved assets. Crucially, it can only use approved content — nothing unapproved ever gets recommended.

Content Retrieval — Approved Assets Only
4

The AI reasons through everything and makes a recommendation

Now — and only now — does the AI (powered by Azure OpenAI GPT-4.1) get involved. It receives the doctor's full context, the rules engine output, and the retrieved content. It then produces: a specific recommended action (e.g., "Send Email"), a priority level, the right timing, which content piece to use, a draft email or call plan personalised to that doctor, a clear rationale explaining why, and a transparent list of assumptions it made.

AI Reasoning — GPT-4.1
5

The rep sees it all in one clean screen — and can act immediately

Everything surfaces in a beautiful interface: the doctor's profile and engagement history on the left, the AI recommendation on the right. The rep can read the rationale, review the draft message, and copy it — all without leaving the screen. The whole process from click to recommendation takes about 5–10 seconds.

Demo Scenarios Built In

Four real-world situations the system handles.

We designed 4 distinct scenarios to showcase the full range of the system's intelligence — from obvious outreach to knowing when to hold back.

Digitally Engaged HCP

A doctor who opens emails, attends webinars, and clicks on content. The system identifies the momentum and recommends a targeted follow-up with the most relevant clinical asset.

Low / Churned HCP

A doctor who hasn't engaged in 7+ months. The system recommends a gentle re-engagement rather than a hard sales push — right tone, right content, right timing.

F2F Preference HCP

A doctor who ignores every email but responds to in-person meetings. The system overrides the default email approach and recommends a face-to-face meeting with a call prep guide.

Compliance Blackout

A doctor flagged after an adverse event report. The system hard-blocks all outreach before the AI even runs — no guess-work, no risk. Compliance rules always win.

What Makes This Different

Three design decisions that set this apart.

🛡️

Rules run before AI — always

Most AI products let the model decide everything. We deliberately separate compliance logic from AI reasoning. Hard rules are code, not prompts. An AI can hallucinate; a rule cannot.

🤫

Sometimes the right answer is "wait"

The system is designed to recommend inaction when inaction is correct. A churned doctor, an over-contacted doctor, a recently met doctor — in each case, the AI may say "don't reach out yet." That restraint is a feature, not a bug.

🔍

Transparent reasoning, always

Every recommendation comes with a rationale, a list of assumptions the AI made, and a list of data it didn't have. The rep always knows why — and can push back intelligently.

📋

Only approved content, ever

In pharma, every piece of communication must go through medical-legal review. The system only retrieves pre-approved assets from the content library. The AI never invents content or suggests unapproved material.

🏥

Clinically grounded data

The 25 synthetic HCPs span real therapy areas Avegen works in — HIV, Cardiovascular, Oncology, Endocrinology, Maternal Health. The profiles reflect how real HCPs actually behave, not generic mock data.

⚡

Built to be practical

No over-engineering. No machine learning model. No complex infrastructure. A lean rules engine + content matching + a single well-designed AI prompt. Simple, explainable, and production-worthy.
