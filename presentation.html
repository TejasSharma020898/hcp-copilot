The Challenge

The problem every pharma sales team faces

Reps manage 100+ doctors across specialties, cities, and countries. Every day they face the same questions — and today, most of them are answered by guesswork.

❓ Which doctor do I contact today?

With 100+ HCPs to manage, reps have no easy way to know who needs attention right now vs. who was just contacted last week.

❓ How does this doctor prefer to be reached?

Sending emails to a doctor who never opens them — or calling someone who only responds in person — wastes time and damages the relationship.

❓ Am I even allowed to contact right now?

Compliance holds, opt-outs, adverse event reporting periods — breaching these rules has serious legal and reputational consequences.

❓ Which approved material should I share?

Content libraries have dozens of assets. Reps default to generic emails rather than matching the right clinical evidence to the right doctor.

❓ Why am I making this recommendation?

When challenged by management, reps cannot easily explain why they chose a specific action. There is no transparent, auditable reasoning.

❓ Should I wait — or act now?

Contacting too soon after a meeting can feel intrusive. Waiting too long after a webinar loses momentum. Timing matters enormously.

Medical analogy

Think of it like a clinical decision support system — the same way a junior doctor checks protocols before prescribing, our system checks rules before recommending. The senior doctor (the rep) always makes the final call.

Step 1 — Safety & Compliance

Rules run first. Always. No exceptions.

Before the AI is even involved, the system runs through a checklist of compliance rules in priority order. Hard blocks are non-negotiable — the AI cannot override them.

1

🚫 Opted out? → Complete stop

Doctor has requested no commercial contact. No outreach via any channel. No exceptions.

2

🚫 Compliance hold active? → Complete stop

Adverse event reported, regulatory review pending, or blackout period in force. Account frozen until medical affairs clears it.

3

⏱ Contacted in last 3 days? → Wait

Minimum gap enforced to avoid over-contacting. Like a physician respecting a patient's need for rest before a follow-up.

4

⚠️ Contacted 4–7 days ago? → Yellow flag

A soft warning is raised. The rep is informed but can still proceed with care.

5

📡 Which channel is appropriate?

System checks the doctor's stated preference AND their actual response history. If they never open emails, it recommends switching to phone or in-person.

✅ All clear?

Only if all rules pass does the system move to Step 2 — finding the right content.

Step 2 — Finding the Right Content

Match approved materials to this specific doctor

The system searches the content library and scores each asset based on how relevant it is to this specific doctor's specialty, recent engagement, and the recommended channel.

🔍 Three matching criteria
1

Therapy area match

A cardiologist only sees cardiovascular content. An HIV specialist only sees HIV content.

2

Channel compatibility

Each asset is tagged with which channels it can be delivered on. An in-person brochure won't be recommended for an email.

3

Relevance scoring

Each asset has a relevance score per therapy area. The top-scoring assets are passed to the AI.

🔒 The golden rule of content

Only content already in the approved library can be shown. The AI cannot:

  • Invent new clinical claims
  • Reference unapproved studies
  • Create new materials on the fly
  • Recommend assets from the wrong therapy area
📎 What gets passed to the AI

The top 2–3 matching assets — their titles, descriptions, and approved snippets — are handed to the AI alongside the full doctor profile.

Step 3 — AI Recommendation

AI reads everything and writes the recommendation

The AI receives the doctor's full profile, their engagement history, the rules output, and the matched content. It then produces a specific, reasoned, ready-to-use recommendation.

📥 What the AI reads
  • Doctor's specialty, hospital, patient volume
  • Engagement score and recent signals
  • Full interaction history (last 5 contacts)
  • Rules engine output (channel, timing, warnings)
  • Top 2–3 matched approved content assets
📤 What the AI produces
  • Next best action (email / call / meeting / webinar invite)
  • Specific approved content to use
  • A short rationale explaining why this recommendation
  • A ready-to-send draft email or structured call plan
  • A list of what we don't know — visible assumptions
🔍 Why we show assumptions

Real-world data is never complete. Rather than pretending otherwise, the system flags what it doesn't know:

  • "No in-person meeting history — channel preference inferred from digital signals only"
  • "Patient volume data is self-reported — actual prescribing may differ"
  • "Engagement score based on 3-month window only"

This builds trust. The rep knows exactly what the system is confident about — and what it is estimating.

Design Decisions & Tradeoffs

Why we built it this way

Every design choice was made deliberately. Here are the key decisions and the reasoning behind them.

🔒 Rules before AI — always

Decision: Compliance logic is coded into hard rules that run before the AI sees anything.

Why: AI models can make mistakes. Compliance cannot be probabilistic. A hard opt-out must be a hard stop — no exceptions, no edge cases.

📚 Approved content library only

Decision: AI can only recommend from pre-approved, tagged assets.

Why: Pharma has strict rules about what can be shared with HCPs. This constraint eliminates the risk of the AI suggesting off-label or unapproved materials.

👁 Transparent rationale always shown

Decision: Every recommendation includes a written explanation of why.

Why: Reps need to trust and understand the output. If a manager asks "why did you contact this doctor?", the rep has a clear, defensible answer.

🔍 Missing data is made visible

Decision: Assumptions and knowledge gaps are listed for every recommendation.

Why: Hiding uncertainty is dangerous. When a rep knows what the system doesn't know, they can apply their own judgement to fill the gap.

🚫 What we deliberately left out

  • No live CRM integration (synthetic data only)
  • No trained predictive model
  • No A/B testing or feedback loops
  • No multi-rep coordination logic

✅ The result

A lean, explainable, compliance-safe prototype that demonstrates the core workflow clearly — without over-engineering.
